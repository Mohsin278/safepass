How to Create a Strong Password

  • Use at least 12 characters: The longer your password, the more secure it is.
  • Mix character types: Include uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.
  • Avoid personal information: Don't use names, birthdays, or any information that could be easily guessed.
  • Don't use common patterns: Avoid keyboard patterns (qwerty), repeating characters (aaa), or sequential numbers (123).
  • Use unique passwords: Never reuse passwords across different accounts.
  • Consider using passphrases: A sequence of random words can be both secure and memorable.

Why Password Strength Matters

Modern computers can attempt billions of password combinations per second. A weak password that seems complex to you might be cracked in seconds by automated hacking tools. Strong passwords are your first line of defense against unauthorized access to your accounts and personal information.

Remember that even the strongest password isn't effective if you share it, write it down in an insecure location, or reuse it across multiple sites. Consider using a password manager to securely store unique, complex passwords for all your accounts.
